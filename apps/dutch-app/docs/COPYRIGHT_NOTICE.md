# Copyright Notice — Chapter Content

## Status: BACKLOG — Pending commercial-use decision

## Concern

The chapter data files in `src/data/chapters/ch1.ts` through `ch18.ts` contain content
extracted from the copyrighted textbook **"Nederlands in gang" (2017)** by Berna de Boer,
Margaret van der Kamp, and Birgit Lijmbach, published by Coutinho.

Specifically, the following elements are reproduced from the book:
- **Dialogue lines** (speaker, Dutch text, English translations)
- **Vocabulary lists** (the specific curated selection and ordering)
- **Exercise prompts and answers**

The following elements were written originally and are NOT from the book:
- Grammar `tips` arrays (detailed English-language learning advice)
- Grammar `explanation` prose (rewritten pedagogical explanations)
- `culture` content (rewritten cultural context)
- Pronunciation guidance and tips

## Risk

Reproducing copyrighted material in a public or commercial app — even if free —
constitutes potential copyright infringement. Charging users increases the risk
and potential damages.

The Spotify audio derivative idea (narrating book-derived text as episodes) would
be an additional reproduction/distribution concern.

## Recommended Actions (when pursuing commercial use)

1. **Replace reproduced content with original material** — keep chapter themes and
   grammar topics (pedagogical concepts, not copyrightable), rewrite dialogues,
   vocabulary, and exercises from scratch.
2. **Or get a license** from the publisher (Coutinho / Berna de Boer et al.).
3. **Or pivot to "bring your own book" model** — app provides tools (SRS, quizzes,
   pronunciation, progress tracking) while users enter their own notes privately.
4. **Or use openly licensed Dutch A2 material** — verify license allows reuse.

## Current Decision

Keep the app for **personal use only** until a decision is made about commercial use.
Track this as a backlog item — see GitHub issue for updates.
